History of Topsham Charter

The name Topsham is an Anglo-Saxon one and means Toppa's Village. Toppa was the local land owner. Topsham was designated a Town when it was granted a Royal Charter in August 1300. The Charter allowed Topsham to hold a weekly market, as it continues to do, and to celebrate Saints days.

In August 2015 the last Carnival Day, which always ended town fayre week, was held. The first Charter Day replaced the Carnival Day in 2016 and our photograph shows our re-enactment of the granting of the Charter to the Earl of Devon.
